Scorekeeper Training

You run the brackets on the computer and manage the clock — you're the engine of the mat. Three short parts, each with a quiz you need to pass before moving on.

How this works: Video → short quiz → next part. Once you pass each quiz, you'll move to the next video. If anything won't load, use the "open directly" link under it.

Part 1 of 3

The Paper Bracket

By the end of this part you should be able to:

  • Read the division, mat number, and time on a paper bracket
  • Match each paper bracket to the system every hour
  • Visually identify the difference between a ready, resting, and not checked in competitor
  • Fill out a completed bracket correctly, including 1st–3rd place names

Part 2 of 3

Running the System

By the end of this part you should be able to:

  • Open the mat and confirm each competitor is present before starting their bracket
  • Trust the computer screen over the TV when selecting fight sides
  • Read referee hand signals for points, submissions, penalties, and DQs
  • Select the winner, choose the correct victory type, and record results on the paper bracket

Part 3 of 3

Starting Brackets

By the end of this part you should be able to:

  • Mark missing competitors immediately when a runner reports them
  • Start only brackets where every competitor is present
  • Identify broken brackets quickly and have the first match ready by 8:45am
  • Understand and follow the break, coverage, and cell phone policy throughout the day
